Use this page for Google Play’s data deletion request URL. White Noise Box is offline-first and does not require an app account. Most data never leaves your phone.

Steps to delete your data

  1. Delete local app data (immediate)
    On Android:
    • Open Settings → Apps → White Noise Box → Storage
    • Tap Clear storage / Clear data
    Or uninstall White Noise Box. This removes local content for that install.
  2. Email a deletion request to Lucky Tools
    Email autoaccentsni@gmail.com with subject “White Noise Box data deletion request”. Include:
    • That you are requesting deletion for White Noise Box by Lucky Tools
    • The Google account email used for Play (only if relevant to purchases or prior contact)
    • Any detail that helps us identify prior correspondence
    We will confirm and delete personal data we hold that we can identify.
  3. Google Play purchases
    Payment records are retained by Google under Google’s policies. Manage subscriptions in Google Play → Payments & subscriptions.
  4. Advertising (free tier)
    AdMob may process device identifiers under Google’s policies. Reset or limit your advertising ID in Android settings.

What is deleted

  • On your device (clear data / uninstall): inputs, lists, preferences, and other local content for that install.
  • From Lucky Tools (after a valid email request): support emails or other personal data we can reasonably identify as yours.

What may be kept

  • Legal / correspondence: limited email records typically up to 24 months, or longer if required by law.
  • Google Play payments: retained by Google — we cannot erase Google’s payment ledger.
  • Ad networks: AdMob retention under Google’s schedules.
  • Device backups: clear manufacturer / Google One backups if they retained app data.

How long requests take

Device deletion is immediate when you clear storage or uninstall. Email requests are normally handled within 30 days (sooner when possible).

No account system: because there is no cloud login for White Noise Box, “account deletion” means removing local app data and any personal data we hold from support contact.
